Privacy Policy for NeonAmp

Last updated: May 27, 2026

NeonAmp respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how NeonAmp handles information when you use the app.

1. Overview

NeonAmp is a local music player designed to help users browse, play, organize, and enjoy their own music library. The app is built around local playback, album browsing, lyrics display, live visual modes, and sharing features.

NeonAmp does not sell, rent, or trade user data.

2. Information We Collect

NeonAmp does not require users to create an account.

The app may access information stored locally on your device only when needed for its core functionality, such as:

- Music files selected or imported by the user
- Album names, artist names, song titles, and metadata contained in local music files
- Album artwork contained in or associated with local music files
- Lyrics or metadata cached locally by the app
- User interface preferences, such as contrast settings or display options

This information is used only to provide the app’s music playback and library features.

3. Local Data Storage

NeonAmp may store certain data locally on your device to improve the app experience, including:

- Music library references
- Cached lyrics
- Cached metadata
- User preferences
- Playback-related settings

This data remains on your device unless you delete the app, clear the app’s cache, or remove the related files.

4. Music Library Access

NeonAmp may request access to files or music selected by the user. This access is used only to allow the app to browse, display, and play the user’s own music.

NeonAmp does not upload your music library to external servers.

5. Sharing Features

NeonAmp may allow users to share songs, album information, or listening moments using the sharing options available on the device.

When you use a share feature, the information you choose to share is handled by the selected third-party app or service, such as Messages, Mail, or social media platforms. NeonAmp does not control how those third-party services process shared information.

6. Analytics and Tracking

NeonAmp does not track users across apps or websites.

NeonAmp does not use third-party advertising trackers.

If analytics are added in the future, this Privacy Policy will be updated to explain what data is collected and how it is used.

7. Third-Party Services

NeonAmp may interact with system-level services provided by Apple, such as local file access, media playback, or sharing tools.

These services are governed by Apple’s own privacy practices and system permissions.

8. Data Sharing

NeonAmp does not sell your personal information.

NeonAmp does not share your personal information with advertisers, data brokers, or third-party marketing companies.

Information may only be shared if required by law or if necessary to comply with a valid legal request.

9. Children’s Privacy

NeonAmp is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children. Since the app does not require accounts or collect personal data through a backend service, no intentional collection of children’s personal information is performed.

10. User Control

Users can control their data by:

- Removing imported music files
- Clearing cached lyrics or metadata if the app provides that option
- Changing app settings
- Deleting the app from the device

Deleting the app may remove locally stored app data from the device.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. Any changes will be reflected by updating the “Last updated” date at the top of this document.
